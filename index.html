📖 How To Use
Welcome to The Fishing Log — your personal fishing diary. Tap any section below to learn how it works.
🎣
Logging a Catch
›

Tap the + Log a Catch button at the top of the home screen to record a new catch.

Fill in the details about your catch — the required fields are Fish Type and Date. Everything else is optional but helps build your stats over time.

  • Fish Type — Start typing and common species will appear as suggestions.
  • Weight — Enter in pounds. Decimal values are fine (e.g. 3.75).
  • Date & Time — Defaults to right now. Check "Date only" if you don't know the exact time.
  • Lure / Bait — Choose from your Tackle Box or type a custom lure below the dropdown.
  • Rod Used — Choose from your Rod Rack. Set a default rod in the Rod Rack to skip this every time.
  • Fished With — Enter who you were fishing with. Separate multiple people with commas (e.g. Dad, Jake). These build your Fishing Buddies list.
  • Location — The specific spot (e.g. "North bank dock"). Previous locations appear as suggestions.
  • City — The nearest city. This is used to look up the exact sunrise and sunset time for your catch.
  • State — Your default state (set in Settings) fills in automatically.
  • Trip Tag — Label this catch as part of a trip (e.g. "Yellowstone 2026"). Use the same tag for every catch on a trip to filter them together.
  • Photo — Tap to upload a photo. After picking one, use the crop position grid to choose which part of the image shows on the card preview.
💡 Tap a catch card on the home screen to view full details, edit any field, or delete it.
🧰
Tackle Box
›

The Tackle Box stores all your lures so you can quickly select one when logging a catch instead of typing it every time.

Each lure has four fields: Lure Name, Color, Brand, and Details. In the catch form dropdown and on the main screen it shows as "Name (Color)" for quick identification.

  • Tap any lure in the list to see all its details.
  • From the detail view you can Edit any field or Delete the lure.
  • Each lure shows a catch count badge so you can see which lures are producing.
💡 If you used a lure not in your Tackle Box, use the "Custom Lure" field on the catch form — it overrides the dropdown for that catch without adding to your box.
🎣
Rod Rack
›

The Rod Rack stores all your rod and reel setups so you can track which rod you used for each catch.

Each rod has three fields: Nickname (shown in lists and dropdowns), Rod (the rod itself), and Reel.

  • Set one rod as your Default and it will pre-fill automatically every time you log a catch.
  • Tap any rod to see full details, edit it, set it as default, or delete it.
  • Each rod shows a catch count so you can see which setup you use most.
💡 The first rod you add automatically becomes the default. You can change it anytime from the rod's detail view.
🏪
Fish Shops
›

Fish Shops is your personal directory of bait shops, tackle stores, and fishing outfitters you've visited or want to visit.

Each shop stores its Name, City, State, Address, Shop Type, Notes, and a Photo.

  • Shop Type options are: Fly Fishing, Ocean Fishing, Bass Fishing, and All.
  • Filter shops by State or Shop Type using the dropdowns at the top of the page.
  • Tap any shop card to see full details, edit, or delete it.
  • Shop data is saved to your Google Sheet so it syncs across all your devices.
❤️
Favorites
›

Favorite any catch by tapping the heart icon on the top-right of the catch card photo. Favorited catches appear in the Favorites section above your catch log on the home screen.

  • The number of favorites shown on the home screen matches your Catch Log Preview setting (set in Settings).
  • If you have more favorites than the limit, View All Favorites and Edit Shown buttons appear below the section.
  • Edit Shown lets you hand-pick exactly which favorites display on the home screen, up to your limit. Tap catches to select or deselect them, then tap Save.
  • Your favorites and pinned selections sync across devices.
💡 You can also favorite/unfavorite a catch from its detail page using the Favorite button.
🧑‍🤝‍🧑
Fishing Buddies
›

Fishing Buddies automatically builds a list of everyone you've fished with, based on the Fished With field when logging catches.

  • Tap the Buddies stat card on the home screen to see your full buddy list.
  • Each buddy shows their name, the date you first fished together, and total catch count — sorted from most to least catches.
  • Tap any buddy to see all the catches you've logged while fishing with them.
  • When logging a catch, the Fished With field suggests names from your history as you type.
💡 You can enter multiple people separated by commas: "Dad, Jake". Each person is tracked individually.
📍
Fishing Spots
›

Fishing Spots automatically tracks every unique location you've entered when logging catches. No setup needed — just fill in the Location field and your spots build themselves.

  • Tap the Spots stat card on the home screen to see all your spots.
  • Each spot shows how many catches you've logged there and the first date you fished it, sorted by catch count.
  • Tap any spot to see all the catches from that location.
  • The Location field auto-suggests previous spots as you type.
🏕️
Trips
›

Trips let you group catches together under a shared label so you can filter and review them as a set.

When logging a catch, enter a trip name in the Trip Tag field (e.g. "Yellowstone 2026" or "Family Summer"). Use the exact same tag for every catch on that trip.

  • Filter your entire home screen by trip using the All Trips dropdown in the filter bar — species stats, state breakdown, and catch log all update to show only that trip's catches.
  • The Trip Tag field suggests your existing trips as you type.
  • Active trip count is shown in the stats strip at the top of the home screen.
💡 Trip tags are freeform text — you can use any naming convention that works for you.
📊
Stats & Filters
›

The stats strip at the top of the home screen shows your totals at a glance — total catches, unique species, biggest fish, states fished, active trips, fishing buddies, and fishing spots.

The By Species section breaks down each fish type with catch count, best weight, and average catch times. For species with a city entered, it shows the exact average time relative to sunrise and sunset.

The By State section shows every state you've fished in with catch counts. Tap any state card to see all catches from that state.

Use the filter bar to narrow down everything on the page at once:

  • All Trips — filter by trip tag
  • All Species — filter by fish type
  • All States — filter by state
  • All Cities — filter by city
  • All Lures — filter by lure used
  • All Months — filter by month across all years
  • Search — searches across all fields
💡 Tap the ✕ Clear button to reset all filters at once.
🌅
Sunrise & Sunset Tracking
›

When you enter a City and State on a catch, the app automatically looks up the exact sunrise and sunset times for that location on the date of the catch.

  • The sunrise and sunset times appear on the catch detail page.
  • A summary line shows where your catch fell — e.g. "☀️ 1h 23m after sunrise · 3h 45m before sunset" or "🌙 47m after sunset".
  • The By Species section shows average catch times relative to sunrise (AM catches) and sunset (PM catches).
  • For old catches without a city, the app uses the state's center coordinates as an estimate, shown as "(est.)".
💡 To backfill old catches with exact times, open the catch → Edit → enter the city → tap outside the field to trigger the lookup → Save.
⚙️
Settings
›
  • Default State — pre-fills the State field every time you log a catch so you don't have to select it manually.
  • Display Name — the name shown at the top of the home screen.
  • Catch Log Preview — how many recent catches to show on the home screen (4, 8, 12, 16, or 20). The same limit applies to favorites. Tap "View All" to see everything.
  • Sync Now — manually forces a sync of your tackle box, rods, favorites, and settings to Google Sheets so they're available on other devices.
💡 Tackle box, rods, favorites, and settings sync automatically in the background a couple of seconds after any change. The "✅ Synced" indicator in the header confirms it worked.
🔄
Cross-Device Sync
›

Your catch data is stored in Google Sheets and available on any device that opens this app with the same Web App URL in config.js.

Your tackle box, rod rack, favorites, pinned favorites, photo crop positions, and settings sync automatically to Sheets in the background whenever you make a change. When you open the app on another device, it pulls the latest version down first.

  • A small 🔄 Syncing… indicator appears under the header when a sync is in progress.
  • ✅ Synced confirms the sync completed successfully.
  • ⚠️ Sync error means the app couldn't reach the server — it will retry automatically on the next change.
  • Use Sync Now in Settings to force an immediate sync before switching devices.
🔗
Sharing with Friends
›

Each person gets their own completely separate fishing log — their own Google Sheet, their own data, their own tackle box and settings. Nothing is shared between logs.

To set up a log for a friend, they need to:

  • Create their own Google Sheet and follow the setup steps in SETUP.md
  • Deploy their own Google Apps Script and get their own Web App URL
  • Enter that URL in their copy of config.js

The app files themselves (index.html, app.js, config.js) are identical for everyone — only the Web App URL in config.js is different per person.
